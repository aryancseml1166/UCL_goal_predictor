Problem Statement

This project predicts high-goal-scorers for UCL using player attacking statistics.

Goal: classify players from shot and attacking behaviour before final goal totals are known.

Research Questions

  • Can pre-game stats reliably predict whether a UCL player will be a high scorer?
  • Which features carry the most predictive power?
  • Which classifier best generalises to unseen player data?

Task Formulation

Binary Classification — Players scoring ≥ 70th-percentile labelled High Scorer (1); rest are Low Scorer (0).

Prediction Scope

  • Input: attempts, on-target shots, assists, dribbles, and match participation stats.
  • Output: class probability and final label (0 or 1).
  • Use case: early scouting support before final season goal totals stabilize.

Practical note: this model supports analyst decisions and should be used with contextual football judgment (role, minutes, opposition, and tactics).

Feature Groups (Compact View)

Shooting Volume: total_attempts, blocked, off_target | Shooting Precision: on_target, shot_accuracy | Attacking Activity: assists, dribbles, offsides, corner_taken | Availability: match_played, match_played_goal

These groups combine opportunity (how often a player shoots), quality (how accurate shots are), and consistency (how regularly the player appears), which together improve class separation.

Conclusion

RF and XGBoost are best performers, clearly above Logistic Regression.

Shot volume + precision are the strongest predictive signals.

shot_accuracy adds clear value as an engineered feature.

Leakage removal was essential for realistic model evaluation.

Next step: hyperparameter tuning + xG/spatial features for further gains.

Key Takeaway

Tree-based models can reliably classify UCL high scorers from shot and participation statistics, making these features strong practical proxies for scoring potential.
